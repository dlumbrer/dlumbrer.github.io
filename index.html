About Me

I embarked on my journey in technology at the age of 17 (since my birthday is in December, just in case). I decided to pursue a degree in Telecommunication Technologies at the University Rey Juan Carlos in Madrid, Spain. I then continued my education by pursuing a Master's Degree in Telecommunication Engineering. However, I yearned for more and decided to venture into the realm of research, ultimately pursuing a PhD focused on data visualization in Extended Reality, with a primary emphasis on data related to software. I am currently in the midst of my PhD journey and hope to complete it soon 😁.

In terms of my technology stack, I have always been inclined towards software development, with a strong focus on web development (which I believe is the most powerful technology today, feel free to challenge my perspective). I am currently working in Virtual Reality (VR), primarily in the Front-End domain using various frameworks and technologies. However, I have a deep-seated passion for understanding every facet of a system, and as part of a startup, I am accustomed to working on the backend, mainly using Python. I am also an advocate for spreading knowledge and contributing to open-source software projects!

Life is not solely about work and technology. I must admit that I am always eager to explore new things and keep learning, especially in the realm of web-related topics. In addition to my tech interests, I have a love for riding motorcycles (formerly owned a Kawasaki ER6N, which I have now sold). I thoroughly enjoy creating gadgets using my 3D printer and tinkering with electronics components like Arduino and Raspberry Pi, armed with just a few cables and a sense of curiosity. Gaming is another passion of mine, spanning from video games (check out my game blog) to retro games (from NES to PSX, not venturing beyond or below that era), and I am an avid board game enthusiast. I find great joy in combining these hobbies with a good cup of coffee or a refreshing beer!

OSS projects

BabiaXR

BabiaXR is a project to create HTML-based 3-D data visualizations suitable for use in XR (virtual reality, extended reality) scenes within the browser. BabiaXR is built on A-Frame and other JavaScript modules (including Three.js), and aims to easy of use and easy integration with other A-Frame components.

Kibana & ElasticSearch Community

Kibana is data visualization plugin for Elasticsearch. It provides visualization capabilities on top of the content indexed on an Elasticsearch cluster. I've contributed to this community, I've developed many plugins of Kibana in order to get new visualizations types and I am always thinking about more plugins.
